abstract: 'One of JWST's most unexpected discoveries is the emergence of ``Little
        Red Dots'' (LRDs), compact sources at z {\ensuremath{\gtrsim}} 3
        with blue rest-frame UV continua, red optical slopes, and broad
        Balmer emission lines that challenge standard models and suggest
        early, unusual active galactic nuclei (AGNs). Using a
        comprehensive photometric selection and public NIRSpec/PRISM
        spectroscopy across six JWST deep fields, we identify a large
        sample of 118 LRDs with high-signal-to-noise-ratio spectra,
        enabling a population-wide analysis of their UV─optical
        continuum and lines. We find clear correlations between rest-
        frame UV─optical color ([0.3{\ensuremath{-}}0.9
        {\ensuremath{\mu}}m]) and slopes; bluer LRDs have blue UV slopes
        ({\ensuremath{\beta}}$_{{\ensuremath{\nu}},UV}$
        {\ensuremath{\sim}} 0.3) and red optical slopes, while redder
        LRDs exhibit redder UV slopes
        ({\ensuremath{\beta}}$_{{\ensuremath{\nu}},UV}$
        {\ensuremath{\sim}} 1.1). The continuum shape shows a similar
        trend; redder LRDs display prominent Balmer breaks and
        curvature, while bluer LRDs follow power-law-like optical
        spectral energy distributions. From literature compilations,
        {\ensuremath{\sim}}60\% of known broad-line AGNs satisfy our LRD
        criteria, and up to 90\% of LRDs show broad Balmer lines.
        Emission line diagnostics reveal a shift from high
        H{\ensuremath{\alpha}}/H{\ensuremath{\beta}} and low [O
        III]{\ensuremath{\lambda}}5007/H{\ensuremath{\beta}} in redder
        LRDs to the opposite in bluer ones, along with stronger narrow-
        line equivalent widths, suggesting a transition from AGN- to
        host-dominated emission. We fit the spectra with a two-component
        model combining a gas-enshrouded black hole (BH) and a galaxy
        host. Redder LRDs require higher-luminosity, unreddened BHs and
        modestly reddened hosts; bluer LRDs require lower-luminosity,
        reddened BHs and dust-free galaxies. This framework reproduces
        the diversity in colors and spectral shape by varying BH
        luminosity, obscuration, and BH-to-host luminosity ratio.'
